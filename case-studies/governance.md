{% include section-title.html label="Impact" %}

{% include copy-block.html copy="How a governance model works/helps..." %}

- It helps communicate the team's capacity.
- It sets user expectations, when requesting updates and iterations.
- It ensures the work we deliver will be stable and reliable on all platforms.
- Define the scope of our team within the design organization.
- Define basic roles and responsibilities of designers and engineers on our team.
- Communicate "how we work" principles to new system team members, product design users, engineering users, product partners, and business partners.
- Manage the flow of work.
- Manage user expectations when making requests of the team.
{% endcomment %}

{% include section-title.html label="Challenge" %}

{% include copy-block.html copy="When Walmart's design system team was new and growing, we needed to establish a defined set of processes that would add structure to our team, and serve as a public SLA (service level agreement) with our users. A few examples of the basic things we needed to define and document:" %}

{% include case-study-h3.html label="Internal" %}

- What does it mean to design, develop and ship a reusable component on all platforms? How long should that take?
- What tools and artifacts will we use in our work? What are the expected deliverables?
- How are the collaborate methods and standards for design/eng/a11y?

{% include case-study-h3.html label="External" %}

- What is the design system team responsible for?
- How do I interact with the team when I need support?
- Will the team commit to my feature request? If no, why not? If yes, how long will it take?

{% include section-title.html label="Learnings" %}

{% include copy-block.html copy="The intricacy of this workflow proved to be challenging in the course of a busy, demanding team sprint. It's important to find a balance between progress and process. Too much process can hinder progress." %}

{% include copy-block.html copy="Tested over time, individual sections of this documentation were helpful tools to introduce new team members to the intricacies of design system work. But, taken as a whole, it was difficult to use as a strict tool for team collaboration. Team members need to be trusted to manage their own individual tasks with a bit more freedom." %}

{% include case-study-h3.html label="Simplified approach" %}

{% include copy-block.html copy="As a design system (and its team) gains maturity, it's easier to embrace the progress-over-process ideal. Individual team members have a more concrete understanding of expectations. It's important that we hit key milestones in our process, but it's more important that we do so with realistic velocity." %}

{% include copy-block.html copy="Defining a process more simply allows the team to adhere to the most fundamental best practices, while not getting bogged down in micro-management of their work." %}
